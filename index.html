🇩🇪 Germany Office

📍 Frankfurt am Main, Germany · Europe Region · Tier 1
Submitted By: Moaz Ghani
March 9, 2026
BT

Business Type

Both inbound and outbound
⚠

Key Challenges

The biggest challenge for our office is the missing operating licences in Germany. Without these licences we cannot:

  • Work with many local partners
  • Cooperate with hotels and travel agencies
  • Participate in larger contracts
  • Prove legally that we can operate locally

This problem is not related to the German authorities. The main step required is the vehicle re-registration process, which needs internal approvals and coordination. Until this is completed, the licences cannot be issued.

Another challenge is that because of this situation it is difficult to grow the team or hire more drivers, since we first need stable operations and confirmed demand.

Additionally, I would also suggest that it would be very helpful if we could organise a meeting with all European managers. This would allow us to know each other better, share experiences from different markets and discuss problems together in order to find solutions more efficiently.

I would also like to request a separate meeting regarding the ORNBUS.com website, because currently it is not very easy to use for partners or customers. Many important features are missing and improvements would help increase bookings and usability.

📈

Q1 Performance

In Q1 (Jan-Feb 2026) our operations in Germany were limited because we still did not receive the required licences for operating locally. Because of this situation we could not expand the local business as planned.

Many partners, hotels and agencies in Germany ask first to see the official licences before they cooperate with us, and at the moment we cannot show them. This issue is not because of the German authorities or because of my side. The main requirement is that the vehicles must be re-registered and transferred correctly so the licences can be issued.

This topic was already discussed several times with Adonis and he is trying his best to solve this situation. However, until now we did not receive a final confirmation from India which is required to proceed.

Because of this limitation, at the moment we mainly operate tours which come from Viator and also some private clients which I built up earlier. These bookings help us to keep some activity running, but the potential of the German market is much bigger if we can operate fully with the licences.

🎯

Q2 Plans

The main priority for Q2 is to receive the operating licences as soon as possible. Once the licences are available, we will immediately focus on expanding the business in Germany. The main initiatives will be:

  • Building partnerships with hotels
  • Collaborating with schools and educational travel groups
  • Working with multiple online platforms and travel marketplaces
  • Expanding our local B2B network
  • Hiring reliable and professional drivers
  • Increasing the number of vehicles if demand grows

Germany, especially Frankfurt, has strong demand for transfers, tours and group transport. With the correct licences we can grow the business much faster.

🚀

Q3 Plans

For Q3 one of the main strategic goals is to expand cooperation with larger international platforms. I am planning to set up a meeting with the company Welcome Pickups, which is already a large transfer platform working with many drivers in Frankfurt.

At the moment they cooperate with multiple independent drivers and partners. The idea would be to discuss a stronger partnership where we could potentially handle a large portion or even all of their Frankfurt operations.

If this collaboration works out successfully, it could bring a significant number of bookings and require us to expand our fleet with additional vans and drivers. This could become a major growth opportunity for our operations in Germany and help establish a stronger presence in the European transfer market.

Strategic Philosophy:

Travel is fundamentally a product business, not just a sales business. Success depends on:

  • Strong destination contracting
  • Competitive air + land packaging
  • Market-specific product design
  • Reliable post-sale operations and profiling the customers

Therefore, both inbound and outbound will operate with dedicated product teams responsible for revenue generation in their respective markets, supported by centralized support in required areas.

Core Idea: Instead of every DMC building its own sales network, we create ONE Global Sales Team. This team sells: UAE DMC, Saudi DMC, Oman DMC, Turkey DMC, Europe DMC - All through one platform (Nexus DMC). Our Destination Management Company (DMC) operates on an asset-light model. We prioritize quality, products, robust contracts, and strategic relationships, all of which are supported by our core technology.

Business Type

Both inbound and outbound
⚠

Key Challenges

1. Production

  • Slow progress in API integrations with major channel managers (Bokun, FareHarbor, TourCMS)
  • Limited number of contracted products currently visible on the Nexus platform
  • Lack of updates regarding integrations with Ventrata and Rezdy
  • Delays in API connections with strategic suppliers (Rail Europe, Europcar, Direct Ferries, Elife)

2. Contracting

  • Growing impatience among partners contracted in late 2025 due to limited product activation and lack of sales
  • Missing solution for suppliers not using channel managers or API integrations (manual upload?)

3. Operations

  • High reliance on custom inclusions, often referencing external platforms such as Project Expedition or Musement
  • Some products on the platform do not yet meet premium standards expected by US travelers
  • Complex internal approval processes (KYC, margin approvals, multicomponent confirmations)
  • Lack of payment autonomy for the EU team (no dedicated operational credit card) - Important also for handling of crisis/emergency situations

4. Sales

Need for a clear European commercial structure, including legal setup, licensing and staffing.

📈

Q1 Performance

1. Production

  • Development of new FIT products from scratch for the US market
  • Expansion of the product portfolio for additional source markets
  • Active sourcing of daily tour operators and premium transfer providers
  • Establishment of cooperation with local DMCs to strengthen tailor-made capabilities and destination expertise

2. Contracting

  • Intensive partner outreach through daily meetings and participation in ITB Berlin
  • 90+ contracts signed with local operators and DMCs
  • 3,000+ contracted products (mostly through API channel managers)

3. Operations

  • Daily operational support for travel advisors from the USA and Australia in cooperation with the Melbourne office
  • Continuous handling of bookings (activities and transfers), payment monitoring and quality checks for upcoming services

4. Delivery

  • Direct on-site support for end customers including handling of emergencies and last-minute requests

Note: For Production/Contracting/Operations and delivery - Proposal of a Nexus Europe DMC development strategy shared with Raj (currently awaiting feedback).

5. Sales

  • Preparation of a Global Sales Europe strategy (awaiting board feedback)
  • Limited activity in France due to legal constraints (absence of legal entity and required licenses)
🎯

Q2 Plans

1. Establishment of a Europe DMC Structure (Key Priority)

The main strategic objective for Q2-Q3 is the development of a dedicated Europe DMC structure. Creating a centralized Europe DMC would significantly improve operational efficiency and support the long-term development of Nexus in the region (both FIT and group business).

Key benefits include:

  • Faster and more efficient production and contracting, including the expansion of hotel partnerships across Europe
  • Strengthening local expertise and on-the-ground presence, improving service quality and responsiveness
  • Greater efficiency in operations and delivery, particularly in handling last-minute requests and managing on-site situations
  • Centralization of key functions within Europe, reducing reliance on other regional teams
  • A Europe-based structure would allow the EU team to manage the majority of tasks locally, avoiding the need to involve other offices for European business (e.g., Australia for proposal preparation or India for product loading)

2. Product Development and Contracting

  • Accelerate the expansion of the European product portfolio (products designed for all source markets)
  • Direct contracting of hotel chains and individual properties according to requirement of all Nexus source markets
  • Continue contracting local operators, DMCs, and premium suppliers across key destinations
  • Ensure that contracted products are progressively made available on the Nexus platform through API integrations or alternative interim solutions
  • Ensure presence of Nexus DMC in Europe (participation in main tourism events in Europe - WTM London, TopResa Paris, Fitur Madrid + some local if possible)

3. Operations and Delivery

For Q2-Q3, the operational focus should shift toward:

  • Efficient handling of confirmed bookings and service delivery
  • Maximization of sales of products (tours, transfers, and hotels) directly contracted with local partners

Key priorities include:

  • Managing the increasing volume of confirmed bookings from the US and Australian markets
  • Ensuring high-quality on-the-ground support for travelers, including assistance with emergencies and last-minute operational requests
  • Strengthening cooperation with local partners and suppliers to ensure seamless service delivery
  • Developing more structured quality monitoring and supplier management processes

A stronger European operational base will ensure faster response times and a higher level of service for end customers.

🚀

Q3 Plans

Alongside the development of the Nexus DMC Europe structure, and following the initial groundwork established for inbound operations, the next step will be to focus on the development of outbound activities.

The details of this initiative are outlined in the Sales Strategy document prepared by Paolina Zunino.

During the first phase of building the outbound structure, the focus should be placed on selected European source markets (e.g., the United Kingdom, Germany, and France).

The sales network should then be gradually expanded by progressively strengthening the sales team, adding new members responsible for sales development in additional destinations.
